# Report Truth Methodology Standard

This standard defines how Cendorq reports, scores, optimization recommendations, and monthly control outputs must stay truthful, evidence-backed, and useful.

## Principle

Cendorq should not sell reports that sound impressive but do not help the customer. Every important claim should be tied to evidence, a clear method, and a practical improvement path.

The method should be useful for any business category Cendorq serves, while still allowing category-specific benchmarks and recommendations.

## Trust Clarity method

Cendorq's baseline method should evaluate whether a business has:

- clear offer and positioning
- observable trust proof
- customer-friendly conversion path
- source-backed authority signals
- practical improvement opportunities
- plan-appropriate next actions

## Evidence requirements

Reports and recommendations should link to evidence such as:

- business identity source
- website or page observation
- offer or service evidence
- trust or proof signal
- customer path observation
- benchmark comparison when available
- recommendation rationale

## Optimization proof

An optimization recommendation should not be treated as ready unless:

- the problem is identified
- evidence is linked
- the recommendation matches the purchased plan scope
- the expected customer impact is stated plainly
- unsupported claims are blocked
- operator review is completed

## Monthly control proof

Monthly control should show:

- what was reviewed
- what changed
- what improved
- what still needs attention
- what should happen next
- what evidence supports the recommendation

## Customer output gates

Before a report or update is sent, the system should require:

- truth review
- evidence review
- methodology review
- customer-safe language review
- delivery approval

## Non-negotiables

- No unsupported report claims.
- No unverified optimization recommendation.
- No customer-facing output without evidence review.
- No private notes in customer-facing reports.
- No benchmark used as proof by itself.
- No AI output treated as truth without source evidence.
- Cendorq remains the source of truth.
