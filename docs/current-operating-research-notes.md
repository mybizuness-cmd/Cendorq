# Current Operating Research Notes

Cendorq decisions should be checked against current official guidance before major changes to search, AI discovery, analytics, performance, advertising readiness, brand filing preparation, security, or deployment posture.

## Current guidance anchors

- Search and AI discovery work best from useful public pages, crawl access, clear visible content, internal links, structured data where appropriate, and strong page experience.
- Public search systems do not guarantee crawling, indexing, serving, rich results, or AI feature inclusion.
- Modern page performance review should track LCP, INP, and CLS across mobile and desktop.
- Campaign reporting should use stable UTM naming so source, medium, campaign, creative, term, campaign ID, and source platform can be understood later.
- Public sharing needs clean metadata, canonical URLs, and share images.
- Advertising readiness needs clean landing pages, crawlable public routes, policy-safe claims, privacy-safe analytics, verified domains where required, and conversion events that do not leak private customer data.
- Meta, search, and other ad crawlers should see the public buyer path, not private dashboards, reports, billing documents, support records, command-center pages, customer files, or protected APIs.
- Traffic attribution should separate organic, referral, paid, email, lifecycle, dashboard-return, and support-return paths without storing sensitive customer content in public analytics payloads.
- Brand filing preparation should use current official filing guidance and qualified legal review. Trademark strategy can prepare names, category language, specimens, use evidence, and filing steps, but cannot guarantee registration or competitor blocking.
- Security posture should track current advisories, dependency updates, runtime exposure, webhook verification, secret rotation, malware containment, and patch urgency without disabling validators to pass.
- Deliverability posture should use recognizable sender identity, SPF, DKIM, DMARC, low-noise transactional content, dashboard mirrors, and safe recovery while avoiding guaranteed inbox-placement claims.
- Customer document posture should keep reports and billing PDFs static, no-leak checked, branded, entitlement-gated or provider-authoritative, and never the only access path.

## Operating rule

Do not move from stale memory. Check current sources, translate them into Cendorq-specific doctrine, validate the build, and keep release review in place.

## Research-to-build rule

Current research only matters when it turns into protected execution. A new current finding should become one or more of:

- doctrine language
- validator coverage
- route-chain execution
- route-chain integrity visibility
- owner manual visibility
- docs index visibility
- dashboard/report/billing/support/customer-safe surface alignment
- no-leak, no-guarantee, no-private-data boundary updates

## Blocked research drift

- Do not use outdated plan names after the public buyer path has changed.
- Do not add homepage clutter because a competitor has many sections.
- Do not expose private mechanics to look more transparent.
- Do not promise ranking, indexing, traffic, AI placement, deliverability, security perfection, trademark registration, competitor blocking, revenue, or customer outcomes.
- Do not make advertising, analytics, crawling, or structured data a reason to expose protected customer surfaces.
- Do not weaken mobile performance, dashboard recovery, report-vault truth, billing authority, support safety, or route-chain validation to move faster.
