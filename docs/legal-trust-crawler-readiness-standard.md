# Legal, Trust, and Crawler Readiness Standard

Cendorq should look trustworthy to customers, search systems, AI engines, ad crawlers, social crawlers, and review systems without weakening conversion or overloading pages with legal language.

## Core rule

Public trust protection should be clear, calm, findable, and properly placed. Do not hide important boundaries, but do not turn high-conversion pages into legal walls.

## Public trust surfaces

Required public trust paths:

- Privacy policy
- Terms of service
- Disclaimer
- Security contact surface
- Support contact surface
- Clear plan boundaries
- Clear no-guarantee language where outcome claims could be misunderstood

## Crawler readiness

Public crawlers should see a coherent, useful business:

- clear company name
- clear product category
- clear plan path
- clear policy pages
- clean metadata
- canonical URLs
- structured data where appropriate
- sitemap with public pages only
- robots guidance that does not expose private areas
- public security contact where appropriate
- useful content instead of keyword stuffing

Private customer areas should not be discovery surfaces.

## Legal boundary placement

Use concise boundary language near claims, plans, reports, and checkout where needed. Use complete language in Terms, Privacy, and Disclaimer pages.

Good placement:

- short plan boundary on Plans page
- short report boundary near report examples or report expectations
- full terms and disclaimer linked from footer and checkout
- privacy and data handling linked from intake and footer
- support and billing boundaries inside dashboard support and billing areas

Avoid:

- legal paragraphs in the hero
- fear-based warnings
- aggressive disclaimers that reduce trust
- hiding important plan limits
- making support language sound defensive

## Dispute-risk reduction

Cendorq should reduce dispute risk through truth, records, and clear scope:

- accurate plan names
- clear plan inclusions and exclusions
- no guaranteed results
- no guaranteed ranking, indexing, AI placement, revenue, security perfection, trademark approval, or deliverability
- clear customer responsibility for provided information
- clear correction path
- clear support path
- billing and subscription clarity
- report confidence and limitation labels
- preserved release records
- provider-authoritative billing state

## Advertising and platform-readiness boundary

Ad platforms and crawlers should find consistent pages, but ad readiness must not create unsafe tracking or overclaiming.

- use policy-safe claims
- use truthful landing pages
- keep tracking privacy-safe
- avoid sensitive customer content in URLs or analytics payloads
- do not route ad crawlers into protected dashboard or report pages
- do not use misleading before/after promises

## Final rule

Cendorq should be easy to trust, easy to crawl, easy to verify, and hard to misunderstand. Trust language should protect the company while strengthening buyer confidence.
