# Cendorq Customer Intake and Payment Timing Standard

Use this standard for Free Scan intake, paid plan checkout, post-payment onboarding, customer questions, missing-information requests, dashboard blockers, and agent work-start rules.

## Core principle

Use progressive intake.

Do not make the customer answer a long questionnaire before they understand the value. Do not collect payment and then leave the customer unsure what to do next.

The correct pattern is:

1. Ask only what is needed to start.
2. Capture the Free Scan submission or paid order while intent is high.
3. Ask deeper plan-specific questions only after the customer chooses the plan.
4. Show any missing information in the dashboard as a clear next step.
5. Let agents begin safe public research when enough information exists.

## Free Scan timing

Free Scan should be short and low-friction.

Ask before submission:

- business name
- website or main public profile
- customer name
- email
- main location
- business type
- main offer
- best customer
- what feels weak right now

Do not ask for full competitor analysis, private access, long history, budgets, internal marketing details, or deep strategy before the Free Scan.

The Free Scan form should feel like one clean action or two light steps, not paid onboarding.

## Paid plan timing

Payment should happen before the heavy paid plan questionnaire.

Reason:

- long questions before payment can lower conversion
- paid work should not begin without an entitlement
- the customer has already shown intent when choosing the plan
- the dashboard can collect missing details immediately after checkout

The right paid flow is:

1. Plan page explains what the plan does and what information may be needed.
2. Customer checks out.
3. Success page confirms purchase and asks the next most important question.
4. Dashboard shows a simple remaining-information checklist.
5. Agents start safe public research with what is available.
6. Missing customer input becomes a visible blocker, not a hidden delay.

## Pre-payment questions

Before payment, ask only light fit questions when they improve plan choice:

- which business is this for
- website
- main goal
- whether they need review, fixing, or ongoing control

Avoid long paid-plan questionnaires before checkout.

Use simple copy:

- After checkout, we will ask a few details so the work starts correctly.
- If anything is missing, your dashboard will show exactly what we need.
- Cendorq can begin safe public research while waiting for your answers.

## Post-payment questions

After payment, ask plan-specific questions in the dashboard and checkout success path.

Every question should explain:

- what we need
- why we need it
- how long it should take
- whether agents can start without it

## Plan-specific intake

### Deep Review

Ask after payment for website, business type, target customer, main offer, main customer action, location or service area, social/profile links when relevant, known competitors, current channels, what the customer has tried, and biggest concern.

Agents may start public research after payment if website, business name, and location are available. Block final report release only if missing information would make the report misleading.

### Build Fix

Ask after payment for approved fix target, approved scope, primary call to action, brand tone, logo/assets when needed, safe access handoff when required, what must not change, and approval contact.

Agents may prepare the fix plan while waiting for assets or access. Block implementation only when access, approval, or required assets are missing.

### Ongoing Control

Ask after payment or subscription activation for monitoring scope, review cadence, current priority, business changes, new offers, competitors to watch, and platforms or channels to watch.

Agents may begin monitoring public signals if business identity, website, and monitoring scope are clear. Block cycle release only when missing information would cause the wrong business, competitor, platform, or priority to be monitored.

## Dashboard rules

Every paid plan should create a dashboard work record immediately after payment.

The dashboard record should show:

- plan purchased
- report or delivery status
- what Cendorq is already doing
- what the customer needs to provide
- missing information checklist
- next expected step
- support path

Use simple status labels:

- Received
- Waiting on your details
- Research started
- In review
- Ready in dashboard
- Needs your approval
- Complete

## Agent work-start rules

Agents should not wait passively when enough safe public information exists.

Agents may start public website review, public profile review, public competitor discovery, AI/search readability review, trust/proof review, offer clarity review, market context mapping, and dashboard report preparation.

Agents must pause or mark blocked when the business identity is unclear, ownership is unclear, required access is missing, scope is not approved, assets are required but missing, or customer context is necessary to avoid a misleading report.

## Conversion rule

The Free Scan should be the lowest-friction front door.

Paid plans should collect payment before deep intake, but immediately show the customer what happens next.

The customer should feel: I paid and Cendorq is already moving. I know exactly what they need from me. I can finish the missing details quickly. My report and work will show in my dashboard.
