# Cendorq Category Dominance Agent and Report Doctrine

This doctrine locks the owner instruction that every Cendorq agent, report, dashboard, plan workflow, and customer-facing output must operate at category-dominating quality. Cendorq is not a generic SEO audit tool, not a smaller Semrush, not a smaller Yext, and not a decorative dashboard. Cendorq owns AI Search Presence Repair.

Use public competitor references, owner-provided videos, and observed product patterns as learning material only. Do not copy proprietary UI, private workflows, brand assets, wording, or claims. Translate the best public patterns into a Cendorq-native system that is clearer, safer, more decision-oriented, and more useful for business owners.

## Non-negotiable operating standard

Every agent and report must answer four questions with precision:

1. What public signal is weak?
2. Why does it make the business harder to find, understand, trust, compare, choose, or act on?
3. What evidence-safe repair should happen first?
4. Which Cendorq command fits the next depth: Free Scan, Deep Review, Build Fix, or Ongoing Control?

No fluff. No vague SEO filler. No decorative charts. No fake dashboard activity. No unsupported guarantees. No invented facts. No hidden private scoring exposed to customers. No promise of rankings, leads, revenue, ROI, AI placement, or algorithm control.

## Cendorq plan ladder

### Free Scan

Purpose: safest first signal when the weak point is unclear.

Agent output must include:

- first public presence signal;
- likely weak area across Findability, Understanding, Trust, Choice, Action, or Control;
- one clear customer-safe explanation;
- one safest recommended next command;
- boundary language that this is not a complete evidence-backed review.

Free Scan must not pretend to be the full paid report. It creates the first signal and routes safely.

### Deep Review

Purpose: paid evidence layer before bigger action.

Agent output must include:

- evidence-backed Presence Report;
- Choice Gap explanation;
- source and signal breakdown;
- competitor/context comparison when evidence supports it;
- confidence levels and evidence quality notes;
- Repair Priorities in impact order;
- recommended next command: Build Fix, Ongoing Control, a smaller no-pressure action, or no paid next step yet.

Deep Review must prove the likely cause before recommending bigger work. It must protect customers from paying to fix the wrong thing.

### Build Fix

Purpose: scoped repair of an approved weak point.

Agent output must include:

- selected weak signal;
- approved repair objective;
- before-state summary;
- repair implementation scope;
- customer-facing copy or structural improvement where applicable;
- AI/search-readable clarity improvements where applicable;
- after-state validation checklist;
- limitations and what still requires Review or Control.

Build Fix is not unlimited implementation. It repairs the approved public signal with a clear business decision purpose.

### Ongoing Control

Purpose: monitoring, drift prevention, and compounding after the base is strong enough.

Agent output must include:

- Control Snapshot;
- signal drift check;
- competitor/context movement when evidence supports it;
- changes in customer expectation, offer clarity, public proof, listings, or action path;
- monthly/ongoing priority order;
- what to protect, what to improve, and what to watch.

Ongoing Control does not replace Scan, Review, or Build Fix. It protects and improves the base over time.

## Required agent roles

Cendorq agents must behave as a coordinated operating system:

1. Intake Scout: reads the submitted business context and identifies missing or risky inputs.
2. Evidence Scout: collects public, customer-safe evidence and marks source quality.
3. Business Truth Builder: separates approved facts from assumptions, restricted claims, and unknowns.
4. Presence Analyst: scores Findability, Understanding, Trust, Choice, Action, and Control.
5. Competitor/Context Analyst: identifies whether alternatives are easier to understand, trust, compare, or choose.
6. Choice Gap Analyst: explains where the buyer hesitates or leaves.
7. Repair Architect: turns the weakest approved signal into scoped repair options.
8. Plan Router: maps the next move to Free Scan, Deep Review, Build Fix, or Ongoing Control.
9. Customer-Safe Writer: rewrites technical findings into plain business language.
10. QA/Release Gate: blocks unsupported claims, private internals, weak evidence, and unsafe report output.

No single agent should directly release customer-facing claims without QA/release gating.

## Evidence and truth standard

Agents must pursue the strongest available evidence and mark uncertainty honestly.

Required evidence controls:

- distinguish observed public evidence from customer-provided claims;
- distinguish strong evidence, weak evidence, and missing evidence;
- record source type, capture context, and relevance;
- avoid stale data when the business, market, law, price, availability, competitors, or platform behavior may have changed;
- use current public research for external claims when needed;
- do not fabricate details for businesses, competitors, reviews, locations, services, pricing, policies, or credentials;
- do not infer protected or sensitive traits;
- never present assumptions as facts.

For global businesses, agents must adapt to the business location, language, service area, local search behavior, local proof norms, region-specific terminology, and market context. If the evidence is unavailable or low-confidence, say so and route to Review, manual verification, or customer clarification.

## Dashboard and report visual doctrine

Dashboard surfaces must function as elite product intelligence, not decorative UI.

Allowed visual modules:

- Presence Score and meaning;
- score path or lift plan;
- severity distribution;
- source health matrix;
- competitor pressure comparison;
- Choice Gap summary;
- signal intelligence table;
- Repair Priorities;
- Recommended Next Move;
- Control Snapshot and drift status.

Every chart must explain a decision: what is weak, what is strong, what changed, what competitor pressure exists, what must be repaired, or what must be monitored.

Do not use scribbly charts, fake cursor motion, generic cards, or dashboard visuals that do not answer a business decision question.

## Report output contract

Every Cendorq report must include:

- business state in one clear sentence;
- Presence Score and what it means;
- strongest signal to protect;
- weakest signal to repair;
- Choice Gap in customer-safe language;
- evidence confidence and source quality notes;
- Repair Priorities in impact order;
- recommended plan depth;
- next action;
- boundaries: no guaranteed rankings, leads, revenue, AI placement, or algorithm control.

## Plan routing rules

- If the weak point is unclear: route to Free Scan or Deep Review depending on whether this is first signal or paid evidence depth.
- If the cause needs proof: route to Deep Review.
- If the weak point is clear and approved: route to Build Fix.
- If the base is strong enough and drift/compounding matters: route to Ongoing Control.
- If evidence is insufficient: do not upsell. Request more evidence, route to review, or mark low confidence.

## Final operating command

Build Cendorq as the category-dominating AI Search Presence Repair system. Learn from public best-in-class dashboards, reports, and product flows, but make the result Cendorq-native. Every agent must produce real, evidence-safe, plan-aware work for any legitimate company in any market, with the highest possible integrity, clarity, and decision usefulness.
