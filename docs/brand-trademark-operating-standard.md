# Brand and Trademark Operating Standard

This standard prepares Cendorq for stronger brand protection without making impossible legal promises.

## Core rule

Cendorq should build a distinctive, ownable, customer-recognizable brand system, but no internal document, public page, report, or owner manual may claim guaranteed trademark registration, guaranteed competitor blocking, guaranteed exclusivity, or legal ownership of a word or phrase in all contexts.

## Current official guidance anchors

- A trademark identifies the source of goods or services and distinguishes them from competitors.
- Trademark protection is tied to specific goods or services, not ownership of a word or phrase in every context.
- Stronger marks are easier to protect than generic or merely descriptive wording.
- A comprehensive clearance search should review federal records, state records where relevant, and broader internet/common-law use before filing.
- Likelihood of confusion is a common refusal risk and depends on similarity of marks and relatedness of goods or services.
- Filing requires accurate owner, mark, goods/services, filing basis, specimen or intent-to-use posture, and fees.
- Qualified legal review should handle filing strategy, clearance interpretation, classes, specimens, refusals, office actions, and enforcement decisions.

## Five owner filing steps

1. Lock candidate mark and brand usage.
   - Confirm the exact mark, logo/wordmark form, public usage, service description, and evidence of use or intent to use.

2. Run clearance review.
   - Search federal trademark records, similar spellings, similar meanings, related services, state/common-law indicators, domain/social use, and competitors.

3. Define goods/services and filing basis.
   - Identify what customers buy from Cendorq, classify the service accurately, and decide whether the mark is already used in commerce or will be filed as intent to use.

4. Prepare filing materials.
   - Gather owner information, mark drawing, specimen or planned specimen, service description, filing basis, classes, dates, and attorney review notes.

5. File, monitor, respond, and maintain.
   - Submit through the official filing system, monitor status, respond to office actions if any, watch for confusing uses, and maintain registration deadlines after approval.

## Cendorq brand moat posture

Cendorq should strengthen protectability by keeping brand assets distinctive, consistent, and visibly tied to the service:

- Cendorq wordmark
- AI Engine Readiness category language
- Scan, Review, Repair, Control path
- AI Readiness Review
- Signal Repair
- Readiness Control
- report-vault and dashboard-command language
- consistent public specimens across homepage, Plans, checkout, dashboard, report PDFs, billing documents, and support messages

## Blocked claims

- guaranteed trademark registration
- guaranteed competitor blocking
- guaranteed legal ownership of generic category words
- guaranteed exclusive use of descriptive service language
- legal advice without qualified legal review
- claiming registration before registration exists
- using TM, SM, or registered-symbol posture in a misleading way
- public claims that Cendorq can stop all competitors from using related concepts

## Owner manual rule

The owner manual may explain filing preparation and the five owner steps, but it must stay clear that trademark filing needs current official guidance and qualified legal review. The system can prepare strategy and materials; it cannot guarantee the government outcome.
