# Cendorq Business Mastery Doctrine

## Category

Cendorq is the business visibility and AI-readiness control system for companies that need to be understood, trusted, and chosen by customers, search, and AI systems.

Cendorq does not sell a generic website audit. Cendorq sells clarity, evidence, readiness, and controlled next action.

## Core customer problem

The customer is not only asking whether their website looks good. They need to know whether the market, AI systems, search engines, prospects, and conversion paths can clearly understand the business and trust it enough to act.

## Money path

1. Free Scan captures real business information and creates the customer path.
2. Review diagnoses what is unclear, weak, missing, risky, or not ready.
3. Repair improves the highest-leverage weakness.
4. Control keeps the business monitored and guided as the market, competitors, search, and AI systems change.

## Conversion law

The public surface should push toward the Free Scan unless the visitor is already known.

- New visitor: Start Free Scan.
- Known customer: Customer Access.
- Active session: Continue to dashboard.
- Unknown access attempt: Free Scan.
- Paid customer: dashboard, report vault, billing, support, and next action.

## Access law

Authentication proves identity. It does not prove Cendorq customer status.

Cendorq must check whether the verified email belongs to a Free Scan, paid plan, report vault item, billing record, or support record before dashboard access.

No blank dashboards. No free-floating accounts. No fake workspace creation.

## Customer-facing language

Use:

- Free Scan
- account
- dashboard
- secure access link
- same email
- plan
- report
- billing
- support
- next step

Avoid:

- customer record
- eligibility
- token exchange
- raw provider payload
- create workspace
- internal orchestration
- backend state
- operator language

## Provider access doctrine

Provider buttons should say Continue with Google, Continue with Microsoft, Continue with Apple, and Continue with Yahoo only when the runtime is genuinely ready.

Before launch, provider access must include:

- real provider configuration
- server-side authorization code exchange
- profile fetch
- verified email check
- existing customer eligibility check
- durable server session
- generic safe failures
- Free Scan fallback for unknown email

Provider buttons must be premium, white, clean, equal prominence, and use current provider identity treatment. They are customer access buttons, not social signup noise.

## Dominance standard

Every surface should answer one question: what is the best next business action?

The system should be simpler for the customer and stricter behind the scenes. The customer should feel clarity and trust. The implementation should enforce evidence, security, entitlement, eligibility, and safe failure.
